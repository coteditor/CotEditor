Edit multiple points in CotEditor on Mac

In multi-cursor editing, the same text is entered at multiple points at once.

You can add your insertion points (cursors/selections) in the following ways:

  • To add an insertion point at a location, click or drag the point while pressing the Command key.

  • To add insertion points at the same column in multiple continuous lines, drag the area while pressing the Option key (rectangular selection).

  • To add an insertion point just above or below the current insertion point, press Control-Shift-↑ or Control-Shift-↓.

To remove one of the multiple insertion points:

  • Command-click the insertion point to remove it.

To end the multi-cursor editing mode:

  • Click or drag somewhere in the editor without modifier keys by setting a single insertion point at the location.

  • Press the Esc key and all insertion points except the top one are discarded.

Multi-cursor editing currently doesn’t work with text entry that requires user selection after typing, such as Japanese. In such cases, multi-cursor editing ends automatically after you type, and the text is entered only at a single insertion point.
